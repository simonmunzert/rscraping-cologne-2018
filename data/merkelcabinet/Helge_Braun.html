Helge Reinhold Braun (born 18 October 1972) is a German politician of the CDU.

He is Chancellery Chief of Staff and Federal Minister for Special Affairs in the Federal Government. He was the Parliamentary Secretary of State for Bureaucracy Reduction and Federal-State Relations at the Chancellery between 2013-2018.

Early life and education[edit]

After doing the Abitur in 1992 at the Liebigschule in Giessen, Braun studied Medicine at the Justus-Liebig University in Giessen. From 2001 until 2009 he was a scientific Assistant at the University Clinic in Giessen.

Political career[edit]

From 1989 until 2007 Braun was a member of the Young Union (JU). He was the district leader of the JU Giessen from 1992 till 1997 and from 1998 until 2001 he was the regional leader of the JU Mittelhessen.

He has been a member of the Christian Democratic Union since 1990.

Member of Parliament[edit]

Braun was a Member of the Bundestag from October 2002 to September 2005. From 2003 until 2005 he was the deputy leader of the Hessian Members of the Bundestag. He also served on the Committee on Education and Research, the Committee on Environment, Nuclear Safety and Nature Conservation and on the Parliamentary Advisory Board for Sustainable Development.

Braun lost his seat in the 2005 federal elections. He was able to re-enter the Bundestag in 2009, winning the constituency of his home town of Gießen[1] with a majority of 59,441 votes or 36.7%. After the election he was again elected the deputy leader of the hessian MPs and was also appointed Parliamentary Secretary of State for Education and Research. In the subsequent federal elections of 2013 and 2017 he was able to defend his seat.

In Merkel's second coalition government, Braun was Parliamentary Secretary of State for Education and Research[2], serving under the successive minister Annette Schaven (2009-2013) and Johanna Wanka (2013). After the 2013 elections, Merkel appointed him Parliamentary Secretary of State for Bureaucracy Reduction and Federal-State Relations[3], serving directly under Merkel in the Federal Chancellery.

After the 2017 elections and the successful coalition talks between the CDU/CSU and SPD in February 2018, Braun became the designated Chancellery Chief of Staff and Federal Minister of Special Affairs in the Fourth Merkel government.[4] On 14 March 2018 he succeeded Peter Altmaier.

Other activities[edit]

  • Loewe Center for Novel Drug Targets against Poverty-Related and Neglected Tropical Infectious Diseases (DRUID), Member of the Advisory Board[5]
  • Friends of the University of Giessen, Member of the Governing Board[6]
  • Sepsis-Stiftung, Member of the Board of Trustees[7]
  • Max Delbrück Center for Molecular Medicine (MDC), Helmholtz Association of German Research Centres, Member of the Board of Trustees (2009-2013)[8]

Personal life[edit]

Braun is a Roman Catholic and is married.
