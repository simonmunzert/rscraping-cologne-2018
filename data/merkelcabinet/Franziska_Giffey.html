Franziska Giffey (born 3 May 1978 in Frankfurt (Oder)) is a German politician (SPD) and the Federal Minister for Family Affairs, Senior Citizens, Women and Youth of the country.[1] She has been District Mayor of Neukölln, Berlin, since 2015.[2]

Early life and education[edit]

During her graduate studies studies, Giffey worked at the Representation of Berlin to the European Union in Brussels in 2003 and at the Parliamentary Assembly of the Council of Europe in Strasbourg in 2005. She received her PhD from the Free University of Berlin in 2010.

Political career[edit]

Giffey joined the Social Democratic Party (SPD) in 2007.

In 2015, Giffey succeeded Heinz Buschkowsky as mayor of the Berlin district of Neukölln, home to many immigrants and high unemployment.[3]

Federal Minister of Family Affairs, 2013–2017[edit]

In the fourth Merkel cabinet, Giffey has been serving as Federal Minister of Family Affairs, Senior Citizens, Women and Youth since March 2018. At the time of her appointment, she had no prior experience at either the state or national level of politics.[4]
